## Big Bang Theory

The [[2_Universe Expansion#Rate of expansion|Universe Expansion]] implies that the universe was much **denser** and **hotter**, and cools as it expands.

Since different particle **reactions** happen at **different temperature**, we can predict what happened at different times.

![[Screenshot 2026-04-03 at 6.43.01 PM.png|400]]<span style="display:inline-block;width:100%;text-align:center;font-style:italic;">OpenStax Astronomy Fig. 29.13</span>

### t~0.001 s - Matter/Anti-matter
Right after the Big Bang, [[1_Lights and Colours#Light as waves|photon]] hot enough to create **matter**/**anti-matter** pairs:
- Protons/anti-protons
- Electrons/anti-electrons
but these quickly annihilate to become photons again.

This creation **stops** as the universe cools and photons **aren't energetic** enough.

There is a slight **excess of matter** over anti-matter, which is one of the greatest mysteries in physics today.

### t~3-4 min - Nucleosynthesis
[[1_Star Formation#Nuclear fusion|Nuclear fusion]] is hot enough to fuse protons and neutrons to create **deuterium**, and these nuclei create **helium**.

The predicted abundance include:
- $75\%$ hydrogen
- $25\%$ helium
- trace amounts of deuterium and $\text{Li}$
which is accurate with measurements.

### t~380,000 yrs - Decoupling
Electrons fly around, interacting with photons. The universe is cool enough for electrons to **combine with nuclei**, resulting in no more free electrons.

Photons will fill the universe and become **redshifted** as it expands.

### Cosmic Microwave
Evidence in **favour of the Big Bang**. A satellite is launched to measure this Cosmic Microwave Background, and measured almost perfect blackbody spectrum.

![[Screenshot 2026-04-03 at 6.53.08 PM.png|300]]

The peak wavelength is redshifted by a **factor of 1000**, meaning that the universe scale has increased by a factor 1000 **since photon decoupling**.

Things we can derive from the CMB include:
- The curvature of space is **flat**
- Matter, including dark matter, is $31.5\%\pm 1.3\%$
- Dark energy is $68.5\%\pm 1.3\%$
- The Hubble constant as derived by the CMB is $H_{0}=67.31\pm 0.96$
