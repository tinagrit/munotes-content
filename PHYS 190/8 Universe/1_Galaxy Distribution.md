## Distribution of Galaxies
Hubble found that the universe is:
- **isotropic**, each area of the sky has about the same number of galaxies
- **homogenous**, distribution of galaxies is the same everywhere

### Cosmic Web
- **Group**, ~$10$'s of galaxies, ~$1$ Mpc
- **Cluster**
	- **Poor**, ~$1,000$'s of galaxies, few Mpc across
	- **Rich**, ~$10,000$'s of galaxies, ~$10$ Mpc across
- **Supercluster**, flat pancake, ~$100$ Mpc wide and ~$10$ Mpc thick
- **Void**, very few galaxies, $100$ Mpc across

> [!tip] $90\%$ of galaxies only occupy $<10\%$ of the volume in the universe

### Cosmology Assumptions
1. The laws of physics apply everywhere
2. The Universe is all that is
3. The universe is isotropic from all positions, and therefore is homogenous
