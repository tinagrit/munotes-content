## Orbit Velocity
The sun moves at $220\text{ km/s}$ around the Milky Way, while one orbit takes around $230$ million years.

 Newton's laws of gravity states that:
$$
v_{\text{orbit}}^{2}=\dfrac{GM_{\text{inside}}}{R_{\text{orbit}}} \implies v_{\text{orbit}}=\sqrt{ \dfrac{GM_{\text{inside}}}{R_{\text{orbit}}} }
$$
This means that:
$$
v_{\text{orbit}}\propto \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{ R_{\text{orbit}} }}
$$
and outer planets are orbiting at a **slower velocity**, the *Keplerian* orbits.

Orbital speeds of gas clouds beyond the edge of the visible stars are, however, **constant** and possibly rising.

Since the velocity is **constant**, we can keep $v_{\text{orbit}}$ and $G$ constant, it turns out that with this distance, there should be **4 times the mass** of the stars.

"Matter" that exists can't be stars. This mass is labelled **dark matter**.

## Dark Matter
90% of the Milky Way's mass. We know of its existence due to **its mass** and interaction with **gravity**, but we have yet to find a way to interact with it.

- Not stars → not luminous
- Not gas
	- HI gas → no 21 cm radio
	- Ionized → no emission lines
	- Molecular → no absorption
- Not dust → does not block light
- Not black hole or neutron star → no X-ray
- Not brown dwarfs → no lensing of halo stars

### Evidence for Dark Matter
1. Rotation curves of galaxies
2. Motion of galaxies in clusters
3. Gravitational lensing in clusters
4. The bullet cluster
