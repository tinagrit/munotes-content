## Hubble's Tuning Fork

![[chart46A.png|700]]

Hubble classified the elliptical galaxies as "**early types**" and other kinds as "**late types**", although they have no relation to their evolution.


### Elliptical galaxies
- Shaped like spheres
- No [[1_The Milky Way#Spiral Arms|spiral arms]], [[4_Molecular Hydrogen#Molecular Clouds|dust]], or [[3_Ionized Hydrogen#Ionized Hydrogen (HII)|HII regions]]
- Many [[3_Star Clusters#Types of clusters|globular cluster]], red stars only, no young stars
- Orbits of stars in **random direction**
- Size:
	- Giant elliptical from $10^{10}$ to $10^{13}\text{ }L_{\odot}$
	- Dwarf elliptical from $10^{8}$ to $10^{9}\text{ }L_{\odot}$
- Denser than Spirals

The sequence of ellipticals **increases** with their **flatness**.

### Spiral galaxies
- Range of [[1_The Milky Way#Bulge|bulge]] and [[1_The Milky Way#Thin disk|disk]] ratios
- Range of how tightly wound the **spiral arms** are
- Blue stars in **spiral arms** and **HII regions**
- $2/3$ of spirals have **bars**
- Stars orbit such that the **arms trail behind orbits**
- Masses range from $10^{9}$ to $10^{12}\text{ M}_{\odot}$
- Smaller than ellipticals

### Irregular galaxies
- **Interactions** with other galaxies
- Intense star formation
- Less massive than spirals ($<10^{9}\text{ M}_{\odot}$)
